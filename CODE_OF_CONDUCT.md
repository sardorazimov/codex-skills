# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

codex-sk is intended to be a professional, contributor-friendly open-source
project. Participants are expected to help keep discussions respectful,
focused, and useful.

Examples of welcome behavior:

- using clear and considerate language
- giving actionable feedback
- accepting corrections gracefully
- making room for new contributors
- focusing criticism on code, design, documentation, or process

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- harassment, insults, or personal attacks
- discriminatory language or conduct
- sustained disruption of technical discussion
- publishing private information without permission
- sexualized language or attention in project spaces

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to project spaces, including issues, pull
requests, discussions, documentation, events, and maintainer-controlled
communication channels.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues or pull requests, restrict
participation, or take other reasonable action when behavior harms the project
or its contributors.

If you experience or observe unacceptable behavior, contact the maintainers. If
the concern involves a maintainer, contact another maintainer when possible.

Reports should include relevant links, context, and any requested privacy
constraints. Maintainers should handle reports with care and avoid unnecessary
disclosure.
